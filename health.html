5 Principles of a Healthy Diet

What's the healthiest way to eat? It depends on who you ask. Many medical and nutrition experts claim to know the "perfect" way to eat for health, yet some of these dietary advocates disagree with each other in some fundamental ways. So, who's right … and who's wrong?

While details may vary from diet to diet, all healthy eating plans have these five principles in common:

  1. Lots of plants: Plant foods — vegetables, fruits, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds — offer a wealth of vitamins and minerals, as well as fiber and healthful compounds called phytochemicals. These natural substances provide health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer activity. Half your plate should be filled with produce for optimal health.
  2. Adequate protein: Research highlights the importance of getting enough protein. Plant-based protein sources such as beans, lentils, soy foods, nuts, seeds, and seafood are excellent choices.
  3. Minimally processed foods: Avoid ultra-processed foods that can cause weight gain and unhealthy shifts in blood sugar and cholesterol. Focus on whole foods like broccoli, apples, and almonds.
  4. Limited saturated fats, added sugars, and sodium: Keep saturated fat intake to less than 10% of daily calories. Limit added sugars and sodium for optimal health.
  5. Balance and variety: Choose a variety of nutrient-dense foods from all food groups to meet your nutrient needs without taking in too many calories.

While everyone needs carbohydrates, fat, and protein, there is no magic ratio to strive for as long as you avoid extremes. To learn more about different diets, check out The Diet Review by Harvard Medical School.
